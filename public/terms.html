Legal Terms : Glossary

All the general terms news about law

Amendment

A change or addition made to a law or constitution.

Appeal

A request to a higher court to review the decision of a lower court.

Attorney

A licensed professional who provides legal advice and representation to clients.

Bankruptcy

A legal process for dealing with debt by dividing assets among creditors and discharging remaining debts.

Breach of Contract

Failure to fulfill the obligations outlined in a contract.

Consumer Forum

A quasi-judicial body established to redress consumer grievances.

Contract

A legally binding agreement between two or more parties that outlines their rights and obligations.

Damages

Monetary compensation awarded to a plaintiff for harm caused by the defendant.

Defendant

The person who is being sued in a legal case.

Defense

The arguments and evidence presented by the defendant in response to a lawsuit or criminal charges.

Dowry

Money, property, or valuables demanded from the bride's family by the groom's family. (Prohibited by law in India)

Estate

The property and assets of a deceased person.

Evidence

Information presented in court to prove or disprove a claim.

FIR (First Information Report)

The initial document filed by the police upon receiving information about a cognizable offense.

Habeas Corpus

A legal writ challenging the legality of a person's imprisonment.

Habeas Corpus Petition

A petition filed in the High Court or Supreme Court challenging the legality of a person's detention.

Injunction

A court order prohibiting or requiring a specific action.

Indian Evidence Act

The law governing the admissibility of evidence in courts in India.

Indian Penal Code (IPC)

The main criminal code of India.

Intellectual Property

Legal rights protecting creations of the mind, such as inventions, literary works, and artistic expressions.

Jury

A group of citizens who hear evidence and decide the facts of a case in a trial.

Judge

A legal official who presides over court proceedings and makes rulings on legal matters.

Jurisdiction

The legal authority of a court to hear and decide a case.

Lawsuit

A formal legal proceeding brought by one party against another to seek remedy for a wrong or enforce a legal right.

Liability

Legal responsibility for harm caused to another person or property.

Lok Adalat

A community court system for resolving disputes through alternative dispute resolution methods.

Maintenance

Financial support paid by one spouse to the other after separation or divorce.

Motor Vehicles Act

A law regulating the use of motor vehicles on Indian roads.

National Lok Adalat

A nationwide campaign for resolving disputes through Lok Adalats.

Negligence

The failure to exercise reasonable care, resulting in harm to another person.

Non-Cognizable Offenses

Offenses that the police cannot investigate without a warrant or court order.

Panchayat

A village council with judicial and administrative powers in rural areas.

Plaintiff

The person who initiates a legal case against another person (defendant).

Precedent

A legal principle established by a court in a previous case that serves as a guide for future cases with similar facts and issues.

Prosecution

The legal process of bringing charges against a person suspected of committing a crime.

Public Interest Litigation (PIL)

A lawsuit filed in a court for the benefit of a group or the public at large.

Regulation

A rule or guideline established by a government agency.

Right to Information (RTI)

A legal right to access information held by public authorities.

Scheduled Offenses

Offenses listed in specific schedules of the Indian Penal Code with harsher penalties.

Settlement

An agreement reached between parties in a legal dispute to resolve the case without going to trial.

Stridhan

Property belonging exclusively to a woman, which she can manage and dispose of independently.

Tort

A civil wrong that causes harm to another person or their property.

Trust

A legal arrangement where assets are held by one person (trustee) for the benefit of another (beneficiary).

Verdict

The decision reached by a jury or judge in a trial.

Wakf

A religious endowment created by Muslims for charitable or religious purposes.

Will

A legal document that outlines how a person's assets will be distributed after their death.
